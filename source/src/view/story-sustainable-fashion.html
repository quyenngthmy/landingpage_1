Together, let's strive for SUSTAINABLE FASHION!

First and foremost,there is a truth we must tell you: fashion may not be 100% sustainable. For what reason? We are gradually yet steadily causing harm to our world through the creation of garments production. Similarly to other fashion trends, sustainable fashion utilizes resources like water and electricity.

NEVERTHELESS, we are making every effort to reduce any adverse effects on the natural surroundings in which we reside. We are starting with reducing harmful processes; we should also use natural breathable fibers that are environmentally friendly, recycled fabrics, more effectively wastewater management, yearly clothing exchange events instead of throwing away clothing in industrial landfills, and numerous other measures.

And one thing for sure: we always aim to create the most comprehensive products possible to meet your needs, in order to somewhat reduce the amount of carbon dioxide emitted by the fashion industry globally.

Environmentally friendly sustainable fashion

Since textiles make up 15% of the total amount of petroleum products, they rank as the second-largest category of petroleum-based items, behind packaging, according to Bloomberg. Worldwide, polyester, a synthetic material primarily sourced from petroleum, is used to make the majority of apparel. The centuries-long dominance of cotton has come to an end with the introduction of synthetic fibers, which have surpassed cotton fibers to become the primary fabric of the twenty-first century. Even so, given that the production of polyester emits 3 times as much carbon dioxide as cotton fiber (282 billion tons) in 2015, some environmental experts refer to the material's presence as a "disaster".

In fact, the entire fashion industry accounts for up to 10% of global carbon dioxide emissions. To put it in perspective, emissions from the fashion industry are even more than international flights and maritime transportation combined (according to a report from the United Nations Environment Program).

"Green" fashion starts with each individual

Additionally, synthetic fabrics such as polyester will generate small plastic particles after each wash and wear. These plastic particles are called microplastics, causing pollution in the oceans, freshwater, and terrestrial environments, inhibiting the development and reproduction of species when absorbed by animals.

Scientists in Australia estimate that between 9.25 and 15.86 million tons of microplastics can be found on the ocean floor. And one of the main causes of environmental pollution is the plastic production process for the garment industry, etc.
